NeRF-RS: Neural Radiance Field for Large-Scale Scene Surface Reconstruction and Shading.

Abstract

With the rise of digital cities, virtual reality (VR) and the metaverse, the demand for large-scale 3D reconstruction has been increasing.

In recent years, Neural Radiance Field (NeRF) has introduced new possibilities for 3D reconstruction. Specifically, in contrast to the widely adopted polygonal mesh scene representation, NeRF utilizes implicit volumetric representations derived from a set of photos, storing both appearance and geometric information of the 3D scene within a neural network. For large-scale scene 3D reconstruction, although NeRF has achieved real-time rendering of a certain quality, the scenes generated still lack precise geometric structures. Additionally, relying on multi-layer perceptrons in the shaders for color inference and volumetric rendering has proven to be computationally intensive, requiring significant computational and storage resources. Due to the fixed rendering network of the scene, editing and relighting cannot be achieved.

To solve these challenging problems, we propose NeRF-RS, a NeRF variant designed for large-scale scene surface reconstruction and shading. For a large-scale scene covering several square kilometers captured by a drone, NeRF-RS divides it into multiple blocks and constructs a signed distance function (SDF) grid for each block. During the two-stage training process, NeRF-RS continually optimizes both geometry and appearance. Subsequently, NeRF-RS extracts the final accurate meshes and bakes diffuse color maps and specular feature maps. NeRF-RS can perform rasterization in Unreal Engine 4 (UE4), allowing for arbitrary editing of the original scene and simulation of rendering effects under different lighting conditions. Experimental results on various datasets demonstrate that NeRF-RS excels in constructing geometric surfaces for large-scale scenes with competitive rendering performance.
